# [Universal - Disputes: Inward International Transfer Disputes](https://app.getguru.com/card/ckxpjk4i/Universal-Disputes-Inward-International-Transfer-Disputes)

<h1 class="ghq-card-content__large-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="LARGE_HEADING">
 Topic Overview
</h1>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph">
 This article outlines the key processes involved in managing international payment queries and provides a framework for Wio Bank’s customer service representatives to operate within.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph ghq-is-empty" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph">
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  The typical processing time for inward international transfers is between 3 and 5 working days. To assist customers experiencing delays:
 </strong>
</p>
<ul class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST">
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  Advise the customer to wait for the standard processing time of 3-5 working days. If the waiting period has passed, customers are encouraged to verify the transaction status with the sending bank.
 </li>
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  Should a customer report that it has been more than 3 - 5 working days without resolution, they will be asked for a transfer reference (SWIFT copy including the UETR number), and their case will be assigned to the SWIFT Payments queue for further investigation if the payment status checks done through PayHub Unity system or FTS and IPI systems, didn't come with a resolution.
 </li>
</ul>
<hr class="ghq-card-content__horizontal-rule" data-ghq-card-content-type="DIVIDER"/>
<h2 class="ghq-card-content__medium-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="MEDIUM_HEADING">
 Key Points
</h2>
<ul class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST">
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
   Wio bank can receive USD, Euros and GBP via SWIFT transfers
  </strong>
 </li>
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  SWIFT transfer usually takes up to 3-5 working days
 </li>
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  If the customer called claiming he is expecting an international inward transfer:
 </li>
 <ul class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST">
  <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
   Advise the customer to wait until the normal timeframes passes (3-5 working days)
  </li>
  <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
   Advise the customer to check with the sender bank if the transfer was recalled already
  </li>
 </ul>
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM">
  If the customer said it is
  <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
   more
  </strong>
  than 3 - 5 working days, and they have checked already with the sender bank, do the following;
 </li>
</ul>
<ol class="ghq-card-content__numbered-list" data-ghq-card-content-type="NUMBERED_LIST" start="1">
 <li class="ghq-card-content__numbered-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="NUMBERED_LIST_ITEM">
  Begin by checking PayHub to determine if a payment has been received but is pending screening or manual credit. If you identify that the payment is pending screening, please assign it to the compliance queue and escalate the matter in the relevant Slack channel.
 </li>
 <li class="ghq-card-content__numbered-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="NUMBERED_LIST_ITEM">
  Next, verify FTS, as the payment may involve a cross-border transfer, which could result in the payment being on hold and needing a manual credit. If you discover the payment on FTS, please assign the case to the AED local payment team to facilitate the manual crediting of the payment.
 </li>
</ol>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph">
 If the payment is not located in either PayHub or FTS, kindly inform the customer that they should follow up with the sender's bank.
</p>

<h2 class="ghq-card-content__medium-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="MEDIUM_HEADING">
 Service Request (SR) Protocol
</h2>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph">
 Wio Care should follow specific protocols while logging service requests related to inward international payments. This includes gathering essential details such as the company name, account number, transaction date, transfer amount, and a description of the issue. Requests are categorized based on the type of inquiry, with clear turnaround times (TAT) established for resolution, generally standard at 7 business days, with extended periods for compliance and technical issues.
</p>
