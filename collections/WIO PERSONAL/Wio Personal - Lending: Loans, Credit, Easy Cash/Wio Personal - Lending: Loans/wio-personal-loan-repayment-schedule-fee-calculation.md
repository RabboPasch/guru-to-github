 Topic Overview of Wio Personal Loan Calculations
</h1>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="CvngZX2sHL7I">

It aims to transparently outline how repayments are structured, the implications of early or late payments, and the overall loan management process.

<h1 class="ghq-card-content__large-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="LARGE_HEADING" id="BSl5uajlPJFh">
 Understanding Loan Flat Rate and Reducing Rate
</h1>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph ghq-is-empty" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="6R9h9ZjP7nL1">
</p>
<h2 class="ghq-card-content__medium-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="MEDIUM_HEADING" id="9PB7xHu4fLBi">
 1. Flat Rate and Reducing Rate Definitions
</h2>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="YTdBrgPQvcHP">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
   Flat Rate:
  </span>
 </strong>
 <br/>
 The flat rate is the annual fixed interest rate multiplied by the total loan amount. For example, if a customer borrows AED 100,000 for 12 months at a flat rate of 5%, they will pay AED 5,000 in interest overall. This means that the interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire duration of the loan, regardless of the amount that has been repaid.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="Cwpg575fgRJ5">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
   Reducing Rate:
  </span>
 </strong>
 <br/>
 The reducing rate is the interest calculated on the outstanding loan balance each month, which decreases as the principal is repaid. For instance, if a customer borrows AED 100,000 at a reducing rate of 10% per annum, the interest is charged on the remaining balance after each payment. This means that as the principal amount decreases, the interest charged also reduces, leading to a lower total interest payment over time compared to a flat rate.
</p>
<ul class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST">
 <li class="ghq-card-content__bulleted-list-item" data-ghq-card-content-type="BULLETED_LIST_ITEM" id="j3Bh7nNs639v">
  <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
   Simply:
  </strong>
  reducing rate means that customer is paying the same outstanding amount every month, however, they are paying higher portion of interest vs lower portion of their principle outstanding.
 </li>
</ul>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="V2jbNKyXL3D2">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
   Variable rate:
  </span>
 </strong>
 <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
 </span>
 A loan account with an interest rate that can change based on economic conditions. Wio Bank only offers flat and reducing rates, not variable rates like other banks.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="htMF7jNInvTV">
 In summary, the flat rate remains constant throughout the loan term, while the reducing rate decreases as the loan balance is paid down.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph ghq-is-empty" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="d3JpXMPX9PAr">

<h1 class="ghq-card-content__large-heading" data-ghq-card-content-type="LARGE_HEADING" id="WJtdUKWZz5tK">
 Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ's)
</h1>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph ghq-is-empty" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="RDfeVmrPR7G1">
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="JJfvZHHGIB92">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
   1. What is the difference between a flat rate and a reducing rate for loans?
  </span>
 </strong>
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="RXP7yD9RkDHT">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  Flat Rate:
 </strong>
 The flat rate is the annual fixed interest rate multiplied by the total loan amount. For example, if a customer borrows AED 100,000 for 12 months at a flat rate of 5%, they will pay AED 5,000 in interest overall. This means that the interest is calculated on the original loan amount for the entire duration of the loan, regardless of the amount that has been repaid.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="h5nw71D0l7aZ">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  Reducing Rate:
 </strong>
 The reducing rate is the interest calculated on the outstanding loan balance each month, which decreases as the principal is repaid. For instance, if a customer borrows AED 100,000 at a reducing rate of 10% per annum, the interest is charged on the remaining balance after each payment. This means that as the principal amount decreases, the interest charged also reduces, leading to a lower total interest payment over time compared to a flat rate.
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="ljB7dLhnqn7z">
 <strong class="ghq-card-content__bold" data-ghq-card-content-type="BOLD">
  <span class="ghq-card-content__text-color" data-ghq-card-content-type="TEXT_COLOR" style="color:#9013fe">
   2. How does the reducing rate affect my monthly payments?
  </span>
 </strong>
</p>
<p class="ghq-card-content__paragraph" data-ghq-card-content-type="paragraph" id="dzXjfRhlX1no">
 With a reducing rate, the monthly payment consists of both principal and interest on the remaining balance. Although the total monthly payment remains constant, the portion that goes towards interest is higher at the beginning of the loan term and decreases over time as the principal is paid down. This means that while you pay the same amount each month, you will pay a larger portion towards interest initially and a smaller portion towards the principal.
</p>
